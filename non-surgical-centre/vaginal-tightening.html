The Science of Vaginal Health

Your pelvic floor is like a supportive hammock of muscles that holds your bladder, uterus, and rectum in place. When these muscles weaken or stretch, several things can happen:

  • Changes in vaginal tightness and sensation

  • Stress incontinence (leaking with coughs, sneezes, or exercise)

  • Reduced sexual satisfaction

  • Discomfort during intimacy

  • Decreased natural lubrication

FemiWand technology uses High-Intensity Focused Ultrasound (HIFU) to stimulate collagen production and tighten tissue from the inside out, addressing these concerns naturally and effectively.

Common Causes of Vaginal Changes

  • Childbirth: Even with the most straightforward delivery, your pelvic floor muscles stretch significantly. While they're designed to recover, sometimes they need extra help returning to their pre-pregnancy strength.

  • Hormonal Changes: Perimenopause and menopause dramatically reduce estrogen production, affecting tissue elasticity, lubrication, and overall vaginal health.

  • Aging: Like all muscles in your body, pelvic floor muscles naturally lose some tone and strength over time.

  • Genetics: Some women are simply predisposed to weaker pelvic floor muscles.

  • Medical Procedures: Certain surgeries or medical treatments can affect vaginal tissue and muscle tone.
